Frequently Asked Questions

  • How do I choose between marble and granite for my kitchen countertops?

    Marble offers a timeless and luxurious look but is more susceptible to staining. Granite is durable and resistant to scratches and heat. Consider your lifestyle and preferences – if you prioritize aesthetics and are willing to invest time in maintenance, marble may be suitable. For a more durable and low-maintenance option, granite is often preferred.

  • Are marble and granite countertops resistant to stains?

    While granite is generally more stain-resistant due to its density, both marble and granite can be susceptible to staining. It's essential to seal the surfaces regularly to create a protective barrier. Wiping up spills promptly and avoiding harsh cleaning agents helps maintain their stain resistance.

  • Can I use marble or granite in my bathroom?

    Yes, both marble and granite are excellent choices for bathroom surfaces. Marble brings an elegant and classic feel, while granite offers durability. Sealing is crucial to protect against water damage and staining. Choose based on your aesthetic preferences, considering the level of maintenance you are willing to undertake.

  • How often should I seal my marble or granite countertops?

    The frequency of sealing depends on the type of stone, its use, and the sealer used. In general, marble may need more frequent sealing (every 3-6 months) compared to granite (every 1-2 years). Regularly testing the water absorbency of your countertops can help determine when resealing is necessary.

  • Can scratches on marble or granite be repaired?

    Minor scratches on both marble and granite can often be polished out by professionals. For deeper scratches or chips, it's advisable to consult with a stone restoration expert who can assess the extent of the damage and recommend appropriate repair techniques.

  • How do I clean and maintain my marble or granite surfaces?

    Clean marble and granite surfaces with a mild, pH-neutral cleanser and a soft cloth. Avoid acidic or abrasive cleaners, as they can damage the stone. Regularly seal the surfaces to protect against stains. Wipe up spills promptly, use cutting boards, and avoid placing hot items directly on the surface to maintain the longevity and beauty of your countertops.
